Sudan Gurung returns as Home Minister; Mahabir Pun appointed as Minister of technology

By Bikash Shrestha | June 9, 2026

Kathmandu, June 9 — Prime Minister Balen Shah has expanded the cabinet today. Sudan Gurung was sworn in as Home Minister and Mahabir Pun as Minister for Science, Technology and Innovation. President Ram Chandra Paudel administered the oath of office and secrecy to both Gurung and Pun. The ceremony was attended by top state officials and parliament members. Gurung had previously served in the same position and was reinstated after clearance from an investigation committee. Similarly, Pun had earlier served as Minister for Education, Science and Technology and later resigned to contest elections. He is an independent MP elected from Myagdi.

Prime Minister Shah stated that the cabinet expansion was aimed at strengthening governance and improving the efficiency of public service delivery. He expressed confidence that both ministers would contribute significantly to their respective sectors and help the government implement its development agenda more effectively. The Prime Minister also emphasized the importance of innovation, security, and institutional reforms in addressing the country's current challenges.

Following the swearing-in ceremony, the newly appointed ministers pledged to work with dedication and transparency. Home Minister Gurung said that maintaining law and order and ensuring public safety would be his top priorities. Meanwhile, Minister Pun highlighted the need to promote technological innovation, research, and digital transformation to support Nepal’s economic growth and create opportunities for young people across the country.

"We are committed to delivering effective governance and ensuring that every citizen benefits from our efforts. Innovation and security will remain key priorities of this government," Prime Minister Balen Shah said during the ceremony.

Political observers believe the appointments could strengthen the government's ability to address pressing national issues, including public security, technological development, and digital transformation. The inclusion of experienced leaders in key ministries is expected to support the government's broader reform agenda and improve coordination among state institutions.

Mahabir Pun's appointment has generated particular interest among educators, researchers, and technology professionals. Widely recognized for his contributions to rural internet connectivity and innovation initiatives, Pun is expected to focus on expanding access to technology, supporting startups, and encouraging scientific research across the country.

Meanwhile, Home Minister Sudan Gurung faces the immediate challenge of maintaining public order and enhancing coordination among security agencies. Officials have indicated that efforts to improve disaster preparedness, border security, and crime prevention will be among the ministry's priorities in the coming months.

"Our goal is to create opportunities through science and technology while ensuring that innovation reaches every corner of the nation," Minister Mahabir Pun said after taking the oath of office.

The cabinet expansion comes at a time when the government is seeking to accelerate development projects and improve public confidence in state institutions. Analysts say the performance of the newly appointed ministers will be closely watched as they begin work on implementing their respective policy agendas.
